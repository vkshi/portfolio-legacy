This is the final part of the larger design project that I accomplished in this class. The idea was to choose an existing or future art biennial and create an identity for the event.

Disclaimer: these designs are NOT in any way affiliated with the Glasgow International Biennial; they were created specifically for this class.

Since the last part of this project was a website design, I wanted to tie it back to the posters I made in the first step. The idea is that once people scan the QR code on those posters, they would lead to this website. They can explore the website at their leisure, which would provide all the necessary information about the biennial. Below was my first sketch of the webpage design, to which my professor commented that it seemed to be very motion-driven. In my final design, I tried to lesson that aspect. These layouts were designed in Figma.
